Benjamin Bloom's new taxonomy will also contribute to my teaching strategies as a teacher by helping me to teach deep learning. Bloom's taxonomy has six levels. They are Remembering, Understanding, Applying, Analyzing, Evaluating, and Creating. Teaching deep learning means using both stages of Bloom's taxonomy, lower-order and higher-order. Using Bloom's taxonomy, students will synthesize the information from their lower-order skills, Remembering and Understanding. The higher-order skills include Applying, Analyzing, Evaluating, and Creating. Students who do not have lower-order skills cannot understand higher-order knowledge. In the Remembering level, the teacher should answer the question: Can students remember, recall, list, memorize, state, define, reproduce, or duplicate the information? At the Understanding level, students must be able to explain, discuss, describe, classify, identify, recognize, select, and locate concepts or ideas. In the Applying level, the students should know how to use, demonstrate, illustrate, interpret, or write the information. At the Analyzing level, students learn to distinguish, compare, contrast, differentiate, criticize, etc., different parts. The Evaluating level will help students to defend, justify, select, and evaluate a decision. At the Creating level, students learn to create, develop, design, write, etc., their products or points of view. All this knowledge will help me with my lesson plans and teaching students.
